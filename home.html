Amazing facts about whales and dolphins

Dive deeper into the world of whales and dolphins and learn more about their lives.

Facts about dolphins

From the orca to the tiny vaquita, learn more about these creatures.

Facts About Dolphins
  • Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals.
  • They can recognize themselves in a mirror, a sign of self-awareness.
  • There are 49 dolphin species, including the orca.
  • Dolphins use echolocation to navigate and hunt underwater.
  • The bottlenose dolphin is the most common and widely known species.
  • They live in social groups called pods and communicate using clicks, whistles, and body movements.

Facts about Whales

Amazing facts about whales, the largest mammals to live on Earth..

Facts About Whales
  • Whales are the largest animals on Earth.
  • The blue whale can grow up to 100 feet long and weigh over 200 tons.
  • They are marine mammals and need to surface for air to breathe.
  • Whales communicate using complex sounds and songs, especially humpback whales.
  • Some whale species migrate thousands of miles each year for breeding and feeding.
  • Whales play a crucial role in maintaining marine ecosystems, contributing to carbon cycling.

Record breakers

Whales and dolphins hold some incredible records.

Whales and Dolphins: Incredible Records
  • The blue whale is the largest animal on Earth, reaching up to 100 feet in length.
  • Dolphins are among the smartest marine mammals, capable of solving complex problems.
  • Whales hold the record for the longest migrations, with the gray whale traveling up to 12,000 miles annually.
  • The bottlenose dolphin is known for its incredible memory, remembering companions even after decades.
  • Humpback whales are famous for their haunting songs, which can travel vast distances underwater.
  • Dolphins and whales use echolocation to navigate and hunt in the dark depths of the ocean.

Brain power

Just how intelligent are whales and dolphins?

Just how intelligent are whales and dolphins?
  • Both whales and dolphins possess remarkable intelligence, rivaling some of the smartest land animals.
  • They have complex brains capable of advanced cognition, problem-solving, and social interaction.
  • Whales and dolphins exhibit self-awareness and can recognize themselves in mirrors.
  • They communicate through sophisticated sound patterns, whistles, and clicks, often referred to as "language."
  • Some species work collaboratively to hunt, showing teamwork and strategic planning.
  • They have been observed teaching young ones and even showing empathy toward injured peers.
